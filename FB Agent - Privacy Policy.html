Here’s how you can generate generic Terms of Service and Privacy Policy and publish them online for free in a simple, step-by-step way:

✅ 1. Generate the Documents

You have two main options:

A. Use Free Online Generators

These are fast, decent-quality starting points. You’ll just fill out some basic info:

  • TermsFeed (free and paid options)

  • FreePrivacyPolicy.com

  • GetTerms.io (has a decent free tier)

  • PrivacyPolicies.com

Each of these will guide you through:

  • What your site/app does

  • Whether you collect personal data

  • Whether you use cookies or third-party tools (like Google Analytics)

  • How users can contact you

TIP: You can generate both a Terms of Service and a Privacy Policy from the same provider to ensure consistency.

B. Use a Template + Customize

If you want more control or you're doing this often:

  • Use a GitHub-hosted TOS/Privacy Policy template (some are even open source).

  • Or ask me — I can generate boilerplate text tailored to your app or site.

✅ 2. Host the Documents for Free

Once you have the documents, you need to make them accessible online. Here are easy, no-cost options:

A. Use GitHub Pages

  • Create a free GitHub account.

  • Create a new repo called something like legal-docs.

  • Add your Terms and Privacy Policy as .md or .html files.

  • Enable GitHub Pages under repo Settings > Pages.

  • Boom — you now have public URLs like https://yourusername.github.io/legal-docs/privacy.html.

Good for: Developer-centric projects, or if you're already using GitHub.

B. Use Google Docs or Dropbox (Quick & Dirty)

  • Upload your policy as a Google Doc or Dropbox file.

  • Set it to “Anyone with the link can view.”

  • Share that link on your website, app, or wherever you need.

Not ideal for public-facing business use, but totally fine for MVPs and soft launches.

C. Use a Free Website Builder

If you already have (or are planning to build) a site on:

  • Carrd (https://carrd.co)

  • Notion (shareable pages, works well with clean formatting)

  • Netlify (free hosting for static sites)

  • WordPress.com (free plan with limited features)

…you can add a page for your Terms and Privacy Policy easily.

✅ 3. Link to Them

Once hosted, make sure to:

  • Add a link in your site/app footer (or wherever you collect user data).

  • Reference them during sign-up or account creation (“By using this service, you agree to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.”)
